LED Safety Light Design Project (2023)

    Collaborated on the engineering of an analog circuit with peers, creating a safety light that blinked an LED at a specific duty cycle in light and activated fully at night. The goal was to design a light sensor switch with a blinking LED circuit indicator, considering factors such as cost, size, and power efficiency. The design process involved creating an astable multivibrator circuit, adjusting resistors and capacitors to control duty cycle and blinking frequency, and addressing challenges like achieving the required minimum current output. The project showcased strengths and weaknesses in real-life applications. While the astable multivibrator offered a cost-effective and reliable solution, challenges such as the need for specific component values and the dependence on positive feedback were identified. The design demonstrated the application of knowledge in a practical scenario, providing a viable solution for an Emergency Super Safety Light Switch.

  • Skills Utilized: Collaborative Design, Analog Circuit Design, Component Analysis, LTSpice Simulation, PCB Design
  • Project Report

Bandpass Filter Design Project (2023)

Collaborated with team members on the Bandpass Filter Design Project to create a circuit addressing the needs of the home healthcare network. The objective was to develop a bandpass filter allowing effective transmission of signals close to 500kHz while blocking frequencies outside the range of 460kHz to 530kHz. The project focused on utilizing components available in our lab kits to design an RLC circuit. We employed LTSpice for initial simulation, confirming the circuit's functionality and subsequently implemented it on a breadboard for practical testing. The project involved extensive measurements, calculations, and analysis of results, with a specific emphasis on meeting power consumption constraints below 5mW.

  • Skills Utilized: Collaborative Design, LTSpice Simulation, Circuit Implementation, Measurement and Analysis
  • Project Report

Microprocessor RSP Project (2023)

Collaborated with a lab partner to construct a "Really Small Processor" (RSP) on a breadboard, featuring an Arithmetic Logic Unit (ALU) and 2 registers. This microprocessor, though simple with only twelve instructions, served as a hands-on learning experience to delve into the fundamentals of microprocessor design. The project aimed to comprehend the basic building blocks of a microprocessor, including registers, buses, and logic circuits. Special emphasis was placed on understanding the role of buses, latches, and tri-state logic, along with the fundamental Latch-Logic-Latch cycle integral to processor design. Additionally, the RSP functioned as an I/O subsystem interfaced with a commercial microprocessor, offering practical experimentation and programming through assembly code.

  • Skills Utilized: Microprocessor Design, Breadboard Construction, Assembly Code Programming, Collaborative Learning
  • Lab Manual

Moving Average Stock Filter Project (2022)

Explored the practical application of a moving average (MA) filter on stock prices. The project focused on applying theoretical concepts from the EE310 course to real-world digital signal processing applications. Leveraging MATLAB, I implemented a moving average filter on historical stock data, with a specific focus on analyzing Google's (GOOG) stock as a case study. The project involved in-depth signal analysis, examining how the MA filter smoothed input signals, attenuated high-frequency components, and served as a low-pass filter to pass low-frequency components. The findings and observations were documented in a comprehensive report.
